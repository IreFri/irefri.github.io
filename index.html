Irene Frizza

Research Engineer developing robust locomotion and control systems for humanoid robots
Ph.D. in Robotics

I design locomotion and control systems for humanoid robots, with a focus on robustness, contact-rich interaction, and adaptive compliance.

My work addresses the challenge of making humanoids walk, recover, and interact reliably in the presence of impacts, uneven terrain, and physical uncertainty.

I build and evaluate solutions using physics-based simulation, control design, and learning-based optimization, with hands-on experience in soft feet, compliant joints, and simulation-to-experiment pipelines.
