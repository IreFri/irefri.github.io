Projects

Selected projects showing how I design, analyze, and validate humanoid locomotion and physical-interaction systems — from compliant hardware to control and learning-based evaluation.

Compliant Leg Joints for Humanoid Locomotion

Problem: Humanoid locomotion is sensitive to impacts and ground uncertainty.
Contribution: Implemented compliant leg joints with parallel and series elasticity and integrated them into an end-to-end simulation pipeline.
Outcome: Evaluated stability, impact absorption, and robustness using learning-based testing in Isaac Gym.

Adaptive Robotic Feet for Dynamic Locomotion

Problem: Rigid feet limit stability during dynamic locomotion and uneven terrain contact.
Contribution: Designed and experimentally validated pneumatically actuated soft feet with compliant structures and sensing.
Outcome: Improved landing robustness and physical interaction behavior in real humanoid experiments.

RGB-D Terrain Perception for Robust Foot Placement

Problem: Robust walking requires reliable foothold selection under uncertain terrain geometry.
Contribution: Built a real-time RGB-D ground scanning pipeline for terrain analysis and foothold planning.
Outcome: Enabled perception-to-action integration for adaptive foot placement in unstructured environments.

Tunable-Stiffness Actuation & Characterization

Problem: Fixed-stiffness actuation limits adaptability in contact-rich robotic interaction.
Contribution: Developed and characterized a tunable-stiffness actuator with modeling and experimental validation.
Outcome: Quantified stiffness modulation range, response time, and repeatability for design decisions.
