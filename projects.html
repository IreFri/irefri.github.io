Selected Projects

Selected projects demonstrating how I build and validate robust locomotion and physical-interaction systems for humanoid robots — spanning control, simulation, hardware prototyping, and real-world experiments.

RGB-D Terrain Scanning for Foothold Planning

Goal: Enable reliable foot placement by converting depth sensing into actionable terrain information.
What I built: A real-time RGB-D ground scanning pipeline for terrain estimation and foothold selection.
How it was validated: Integrated perception + planning with locomotion, focusing on latency, robustness, and deployment constraints.

Adaptive Soft Feet for Stable Landing and Terrain Interaction

Goal: Increase landing stability and reduce impact sensitivity during dynamic locomotion.
What I built: Pneumatically actuated soft feet with tunable stiffness, compliant structure, and sensing for contact-aware behavior.
How it was validated: Experimental evaluation on humanoid setups focusing on stability, contact behavior, and repeatability.

Soft Robotics Mechanism Design Pneumatics Control Experiments

Compliant Leg Joints for Robust Humanoid Locomotion

Goal: Improve robustness to impacts and terrain uncertainty during humanoid locomotion.
What I built: A compliant joint model with parallel and series elasticity integrated into a full simulation pipeline.
How it was validated: Stress-tested stability and recovery using learning-based testing in Isaac Gym (large-scale rollouts and perturbations).

Tunable-Stiffness Actuation: Design, Modeling, and Test

Goal: Improve adaptability in contact tasks through stiffness modulation at the actuator level.
What I built: A tunable-stiffness actuator and a characterization workflow (modeling + experiments).
How it was validated: Quantified stiffness range, response time, and repeatability to support design trade-offs.
